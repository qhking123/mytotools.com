How to Convert 164 Centimeters to Feet

Converting centimeters to feet is a common task, especially when dealing with international measurements. Here's a step-by-step guide to convert 164 cm to feet.

Step 1: Understand the Conversion Factor

The fundamental conversion factor you need to know is that 1 foot is equal to 30.48 centimeters. Conversely, 1 centimeter is approximately 0.0328084 feet.

Formula: $1 \text{ foot} = 30.48 \text{ cm}$

Or: $1 \text{ cm} = \frac{1}{30.48} \text{ feet} \approx 0.0328084 \text{ feet}$

Step 2: Convert Centimeters to Feet

To convert 164 cm to feet, divide 164 by the conversion factor (30.48 cm/foot).

Formula: $\text{Feet} = \frac{\text{Centimeters}}{\text{30.48}}$

Calculation: $\text{Feet} = \frac{164}{30.48} \approx 5.379 \text{ feet}$

Step 3: Convert the Decimal Part of Feet to Inches

Since feet are often expressed in feet and inches, we need to convert the decimal part of the feet into inches. There are 12 inches in 1 foot.

Formula: $\text{Inches (from decimal)} = \text{Decimal part of feet} \times 12$

From Step 2, the total feet is approximately 5.379 feet. The whole number is 5 feet. The decimal part is $0.379$.

Calculation: $\text{Inches} = 0.379 \times 12 \approx 4.548 \text{ inches}$

Step 4: Combine Feet and Inches

Now, combine the whole number of feet from Step 2 with the calculated inches from Step 3.

Result: $164 \text{ cm} \approx 5 \text{ feet and } 4.55 \text{ inches}$

Centimeter (cm)

The centimeter (cm) is a unit of length in the metric system, equal to one hundredth of a meter. It is widely used around the world for everyday measurements.

History and Origin

The metric system, from which the centimeter derives, was first adopted in France in the late 18th century during the French Revolution. It was designed to be a universal and coherent system of measurement, based on natural phenomena rather than arbitrary standards. The meter was originally defined as one ten-millionth of the distance from the North Pole to the Equator along the meridian passing through Paris.

Current Usage

Centimeters are a practical unit for many applications, including:

  • Measuring height and clothing sizes.
  • Dimensions of small to medium-sized objects (e.g., paper, books, small electronics).
  • In scientific and medical fields for precision measurements.
  • In construction and engineering, particularly in metric-system countries.

Foot (ft)

The foot (ft) is a unit of length in the imperial and U.S. customary systems of measurement. It is defined as exactly 0.3048 meters.

History and Origin

The foot as a unit of length has a long and varied history, with its origins dating back to ancient civilizations. Historically, it was literally based on the length of a human foot, though its exact length varied considerably by region and over time. Different "feet" existed across Europe before standardization.

The modern international foot, used in the United States and the United Kingdom, was defined in 1959 by an international agreement as precisely 0.3048 meters.

Current Usage

The foot is primarily used in:

  • The United States for most everyday measurements, including height, distance, and construction.
  • The United Kingdom and Canada, though these countries have largely adopted the metric system, feet and inches are still commonly used for height and some other traditional measurements.
  • Aviation and sailing for altitude and depth measurements.

Important Considerations When Converting 164 cm to Feet

  • Precision: Depending on the context, the level of precision needed for the conversion can vary. For everyday use, "5 feet 4.5 inches" is usually sufficient. For engineering or scientific applications, more decimal places (e.g., 5.379265 feet) might be required.
  • Rounding: Be mindful of how you round your final answer. Rounding to too few decimal places can introduce errors, while too many might be unnecessary.
  • Context: Understand why you are converting. Is it for a legal document, a casual conversation, or a precise measurement? The context often dictates the appropriate format (e.g., decimal feet vs. feet and inches).
  • Common Misconception: A common mistake is to simply take the decimal part of the feet and treat it as inches (e.g., assuming 5.37 feet means 5 feet and 3.7 inches). Remember, the decimal part of feet must be multiplied by 12 to get inches.

Common Misconceptions and Errors in 164 cm to Feet Conversion

  • Direct Decimal Interpretation: The most common mistake is assuming that the decimal part of a foot measurement directly represents inches. For example, $5.5 \text{ feet}$ is not $5 \text{ feet } 5 \text{ inches}$, but rather $5 \text{ feet } 6 \text{ inches}$ (since $0.5 \times 12 = 6$).
  • Incorrect Conversion Factor: Using an imprecise or incorrect conversion factor (e.g., $1 \text{ cm} = 0.03 \text{ feet}$ instead of $0.0328084 \text{ feet}$) can lead to inaccuracies, especially over larger measurements.
  • Forgetting to Convert Decimal to Inches: Some people convert to decimal feet and stop there, forgetting the final step of converting the fractional part into inches for a more intuitive "feet and inches" reading.
  • Rounding Too Early: Rounding intermediate calculation steps can compound errors. It's best to carry more decimal places during calculations and round only the final result.
