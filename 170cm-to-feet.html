170 Centimeters to Feet Conversion

Converting centimeters to feet is a common task, especially when dealing with international measurements. Here's a step-by-step guide to convert 170 cm into feet and inches.

How to Convert 170 cm to Feet: A Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Understand the Conversion Factor

The fundamental conversion factor between centimeters and feet is that 1 foot is equal to 30.48 centimeters. This is the bedrock of our conversion.

1 foot = 30.48 cm

Step 2: Convert Centimeters to Feet (Decimal)

To convert 170 cm to feet, divide the number of centimeters by the conversion factor (30.48).

Feet (decimal) = Centimeters / 30.48
Feet (decimal) = 170 cm / 30.48 cm/foot ≈ 5.5775 feet

Step 3: Extract the Whole Feet and Remaining Decimal

From the decimal feet value, the whole number represents the full feet. The decimal part needs to be converted into inches.

Whole Feet = Floor(5.5775) = 5 feet
Remaining Decimal = 5.5775 - 5 = 0.5775 feet

Step 4: Convert the Remaining Decimal to Inches

Since 1 foot is equal to 12 inches, multiply the remaining decimal part by 12 to get the inches.

Inches = Remaining Decimal * 12
Inches = 0.5775 feet * 12 inches/foot ≈ 6.93 inches

Step 5: Combine Feet and Inches

Now, combine the whole feet with the calculated inches to get the final conversion.

170 cm ≈ 5 feet and 6.93 inches (5' 6.93")

What is a Centimeter?

A centimeter (symbol: cm) is a unit of length in the metric system, equal to one hundredth of a meter. It is the second-smallest unit of length in the metric system after the millimeter, and is widely used for everyday measurements.

History of the Centimeter

The metric system, from which the centimeter originates, was first adopted in France in the late 18th century during the French Revolution. It was conceived as a universal system of measurement, based on natural units rather than arbitrary standards. The meter was originally defined as one ten-millionth of the distance from the North Pole to the Equator along the meridian passing through Paris. The centimeter, as a derived unit, naturally followed this definition as 1/100th of a meter.

Current Usage of the Centimeter

Today, the centimeter is used globally for various purposes, particularly in countries that have adopted the metric system. Its applications include:

  • Measuring Height: Commonly used to measure a person's height, especially in Europe, Asia, and other metric-using regions.
  • Clothing and Textiles: Dimensions for fabric, clothing sizes, and patterns are often given in centimeters.
  • Small Object Measurement: Ideal for measuring small to medium-sized objects in daily life, such as the length of a pencil, the width of a book, or the dimensions of a photo frame.
  • Medical Field: Used for various measurements in medicine, like wound size, infant length, or medical imaging dimensions.
  • Engineering and Design: Employed in technical drawings, blueprints, and design specifications where precision for smaller components is required.

What is a Foot?

A foot (plural: feet; symbol: ft or ′) is a unit of length in the imperial and U.S. customary systems of measurement. It is equal to 12 inches or exactly 30.48 centimeters.

History of the Foot

The foot as a unit of measurement has ancient origins, appearing in various forms across many civilizations throughout history. Its exact length varied significantly by region and over time. Historically, the "foot" was often based on the actual length of a human foot, though this was not standardized. Ancient Roman, Greek, and Egyptian cultures all had their own versions of the foot. The modern definition of the international foot (exactly 30.48 cm) was agreed upon in 1959 by the United States and countries of the Commonwealth of Nations. This standardized definition was a result of efforts to reconcile different national standards and facilitate international trade and scientific cooperation.

Current Usage of the Foot

Despite the global prevalence of the metric system, the foot remains widely used in certain contexts, particularly in the United States and, to some extent, in the United Kingdom and Canada. Its common applications include:

  • Measuring Height: Predominantly used for expressing human height, especially in the U.S. (e.g., 5 feet 10 inches).
  • Aviation: Aircraft altitudes are almost universally measured in feet across the world.
  • Construction and Architecture: Building dimensions, room sizes, and material lengths are frequently measured in feet and inches in countries using the imperial system.
  • Real Estate: Property sizes and room dimensions are often advertised in square feet.
  • Sports: Distances and heights in many sports, especially those originating in imperial-system countries (like American football, basketball, and track and field in the U.S.), are often measured in feet.

Common Misconceptions and Errors in 170 cm to Feet Conversion

Despite its straightforward nature, several common misconceptions and errors can arise during the conversion of 170 cm to feet:

  • Direct Decimal Conversion: A common mistake is to simply state the decimal feet (e.g., 5.58 feet) instead of converting the decimal part into inches. While mathematically correct, "5.58 feet" is not how height is typically expressed in the imperial system. It should always be broken down into feet and inches (e.g., 5 feet 7 inches).
  • Incorrect Conversion Factor: Using an imprecise or incorrect conversion factor (e.g., 1 foot = 30 cm instead of 30.48 cm) can lead to slight but significant inaccuracies, especially in professional contexts.
  • Rounding Errors: Premature or improper rounding during intermediate steps can accumulate errors, leading to a final result that is less accurate than necessary. It's best to keep more decimal places during calculation and round only the final inch value.
  • Confusing Feet and Inches: Sometimes, people might mistakenly interchange feet and inches, or miscalculate the conversion of the decimal remainder into inches (e.g., thinking 0.5 feet is 5 inches instead of 6 inches).
