168 Centimeters to Feet: A Comprehensive Guide

Converting 168 cm to Feet: Step-by-Step

Converting centimeters to feet involves a simple mathematical process. Here's how to do it in four easy steps:

Step 1: Understand the Conversion Factor

The fundamental conversion factor you need to remember is that 1 foot = 30.48 centimeters. Conversely, 1 centimeter ≈ 0.0328084 feet.

1 cm = 1 / 30.48 ft

Step 2: Divide Centimeters by the Conversion Factor

To convert a given value in centimeters to feet, you divide the centimeter value by 30.48.

Feet = Centimeters / 30.48

Step 3: Calculate the Decimal Feet Value

For 168 cm, the calculation is:

Feet = 168 / 30.48 ≈ 5.5118 ft

Step 4: Convert the Decimal Part to Inches (Optional but Recommended)

Since 1 foot equals 12 inches, you can convert the decimal part of the feet value into inches for a more precise measurement in feet and inches. Take the decimal part (0.5118) and multiply it by 12.

Inches = 0.5118 * 12 ≈ 6.1416 inches

So, 168 centimeters is approximately 5 feet and 6.14 inches.

About the Centimeter (cm)

The centimeter (symbol: cm) is a unit of length in the metric system, equal to one hundredth of a meter. It is part of the International System of Units (SI).

Historical Origin

The metric system, from which the centimeter derives, was conceived in France during the late 18th century. It aimed to create a rational, universal, and consistent system of measurement, replacing the disparate and often confusing local units. The meter was originally defined as one ten-millionth of the distance from the North Pole to the Equator along the meridian passing through Paris.

Current Usage

  • Everyday Measurements: Centimeters are widely used for everyday measurements of length, such as measuring height, fabric, paper, and small objects.
  • Scientific and Technical Fields: While meters and millimeters are often preferred for precision, centimeters are still used in various scientific and technical applications where they provide a convenient scale.
  • International Standard: As part of the SI, the centimeter is recognized and used globally, facilitating international trade, science, and communication.
  • Clothing and Body Measurements: Often used in fashion for body measurements and garment sizing.

About the Foot (ft)

The foot (plural: feet; abbreviation: ft) is a unit of length in the imperial and U.S. customary systems of measurement. It is defined as exactly 0.3048 meters.

Historical Origin

The foot has ancient origins and has been used as a unit of length in many different systems of measurement throughout history, including Greek, Roman, Chinese, French, and English systems. Its length varied considerably from region to region and over time. The "foot" likely originated from the length of a human foot, though this varied from person to person. The current definition of the international foot (0.3048 meters) was agreed upon in 1959 by the United States and countries of the Commonwealth of Nations.

Current Usage

  • United States: The foot remains a primary unit of length in the United States for various purposes, including height, elevation, construction, and property measurements.
  • United Kingdom (Limited): While the UK officially adopted the metric system, feet and inches are still commonly used for measuring people's height and for some road signs.
  • Aviation: Altitude in aviation is almost universally measured in feet across the globe.
  • Sports: Many sports, particularly those originating from English-speaking countries, use feet and inches for distances and heights (e.g., track and field, basketball hoop height).

Important Considerations for 168 cm to Feet Conversion

  • Precision Requirements: The level of precision needed depends on the context. For casual conversation, "5 feet 6 inches" might suffice, but for engineering or medical applications, more decimal places might be necessary.
  • Rounding: Be mindful of rounding errors. If converting back and forth, slight discrepancies can accumulate. It's often best to keep more decimal places during intermediate steps and round only at the final result.
  • Context Matters: Always consider the audience and the purpose of the conversion. Using the appropriate units for the specific context prevents confusion.
  • Standard vs. Historical Feet: Be aware that historically, the length of a "foot" varied. Always assume the international foot (0.3048 meters) unless otherwise specified.

Common Misconceptions and Errors in 168 cm to Feet Conversion

  • Direct Conversion of Centimeters to Inches: A common mistake is directly converting centimeters to inches without first converting to feet. Remember, there are 12 inches in a foot, and 1 inch is 2.54 cm.
  • Incorrect Conversion Factor: Using an imprecise conversion factor or mistakenly using the inverse (e.g., multiplying by 30.48 instead of dividing, or vice-versa).
  • Ignoring the Decimal Part for Inches: Often, people correctly convert to decimal feet but then round to the nearest whole foot, ignoring the significant decimal part that represents inches. For example, converting 168 cm to 5.51 feet and stating it as "5 feet" instead of "5 feet 6 inches".
  • Misinterpreting "Feet" and "Feet and Inches": Not understanding the difference between a height expressed solely in decimal feet (e.g., 5.51 ft) and the more common "feet and inches" format (e.g., 5' 6").
  • Rounding Too Early: Rounding off numbers too early in a multi-step calculation can lead to accumulated errors in the final result. It's best to perform calculations with high precision and round only the final answer.
